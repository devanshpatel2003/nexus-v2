# Initial A100/H100 Export Restrictions
doc_id: event_2022-08-31
date: 2022-08-31
severity: High
event_type: New Restriction
tickers: NVDA, AMD
source: SEC 8-K Filing, Reuters

## Summary
US government notifies Nvidia that A100 and H100 chips require export licenses for China and Russia. This is the first major restriction on AI accelerators, signaling a new era of tech decoupling.

## Chips Affected
A100, H100

## Countries Targeted
China, Russia

## Market Impact
- NVDA 5-day reaction: -6.6%
- AMD 5-day reaction: -3.2%
- Nvidia disclosed approximately $400M potential Q3 revenue impact

## Significance
This was the opening salvo in what became a sustained campaign of US export controls targeting advanced AI chips. It established the precedent that specific chip architectures could be restricted based on compute performance thresholds.
